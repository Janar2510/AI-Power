# Foundry One Brand System

## Brand Summary

- Working Product Name: **Foundry One**
- Descriptor: AI-powered modular ERP
- Internal Repository Name: `erp-platform`
- Brand Character: Industrial executive
- Visual Style: Subtle professional skeuomorphism

## Why This Name

`Foundry One` suggests making, structuring, and forging many operational disciplines into one coherent system. It feels premium and infrastructural without sounding playful, consumer-facing, or derivative of Odoo branding.

## Naming Criteria

- Must feel credible in finance, operations, inventory, HR, and CRM contexts.
- Must support a suite of modules and AI-assisted workflows.
- Must sound premium and durable rather than startup-casual.
- Must work in UI chrome, documentation, sales copy, and dashboard branding.
- Must not imply only one vertical or only accounting.

## Alternate Shortlist

- Forge Ledger
- North Foundry
- Atlas Works

## Brand Pillars

### Precision

The product should feel exact, reliable, and controlled. Typography, alignment, and state feedback must support trust in transactional work.

### Craft

The interface should feel intentionally made. Materials, edges, depth, and motion should suggest a refined tool, not a generic dashboard template.

### Control

Users need fast orientation, predictable navigation, and immediate visibility into what is active, pending, risky, or complete.

### Calm Density

The platform handles dense information, but the experience should still feel breathable and composed over long work sessions.

## Audience

- Operations teams
- Finance and accounting users
- Sales and CRM teams
- Inventory and manufacturing staff
- Administrators configuring company-wide workflows

## Tone of Voice

- Clear
- Assured
- Technical without jargon overload
- Premium without sounding luxurious for its own sake
- Helpful, direct, and operational

## Typography Direction

- Display typography should feel architectural and controlled.
- UI typography should optimize scanability, numeric legibility, and long-session comfort.
- Use tabular numerals for KPIs, tables, monetary values, and record counts.
- Headings should feel machined and condensed in attitude, not playful or editorial.

## Color Direction

- Base palette should center on graphite, steel, smoke, porcelain, ink, and restrained signal tones.
- Primary accents should feel engineered, not neon.
- Success, warning, danger, and info colors must remain highly legible in dense enterprise contexts.
- Dark mode should feel like smoked metal and glass, not generic blue-black.
- Light mode should feel like crafted paper, polished composite, and soft daylight, not stark white.

## Material Language

- Sidebar and navbar: smoked glass over machined structural surfaces
- Cards and sheets: layered composite or engineered paper with subtle edge light
- Inputs: inset surfaces with crisp focus states
- Buttons: raised primary actions, restrained secondary actions, pressed-state realism
- Overlays and drawers: heavier depth, dimmed backdrops, and higher contrast separators

## Icon and Logo Direction

- Favor geometric, low-ornament iconography.
- Avoid playful mascots or whimsical symbols.
- Wordmark should work in monochrome and token-based color variants.
- Use a symbol direction based on forge marks, grid intersections, or structural joints rather than abstract startup swooshes.

## Brand Usage in Product

- Navbar brand should use the working name `Foundry One`.
- Documentation should refer to the product as `Foundry One` and optionally include "AI-powered modular ERP" on first mention.
- Module surfaces should stay subordinate to the master brand; use module accents sparingly.

## Non-Goals

- No imitation of Odoo branding, names, or logo shapes.
- No faux-retro skeuomorphism such as leather stitching, extreme bevels, or novelty chrome.
- No luxury-for-luxury styling that harms data clarity or performance perception.
